## Mystic

Building Agatha Crane with a Mystic main class (from The Drowned City expansion) changes her from a standard, clue-hunting Seeker into a fascinating, high-tempo spellcaster.

Her core engine relies on a strict rhythm: Manipulate the chaos bag (by sealing, canceling, or ignoring tokens) to trigger her reaction, which allows you to replay an Insight or Spell event from your discard pile at the end of your turn. Because she has access to Spell events up to Level 3 from any class, Mystic Agatha can pull off devastating combinations.  

Two distinct, highly effective archetype recommendations for Mystic Agatha leverage this unique card pool:

## 1. The "Dreamer" Flex Cluever (Recommended)

This deck turns Agatha into an incredibly stable, high-willpower clue-finder who can protect herself and pivot to fighting when necessary. It relies on the new Drowned City suite designed specifically to trigger her ability.

### The Core Engine
* Asset Suite: Eyes of the Dreamer (Investigate) and Breath of the Sleeper (Fight). These assets let you look at multiple chaos tokens and "ignore" the ones you don't choose. By ignoring a token, you automatically satisfy Agatha's reaction text, fueling your end-of-turn event recursion every single round.  
* The Economy: Mortar and Pestle + Robes of Endless Night. Replaying events every turn gets expensive. Mortar and Pestle generates resources when you or allies discover clues, and the Robes lower the cost of your spell events.  
* Allies: Katarina Sojka. She synergizes heavily with ignoring tokens, offering a backup trigger to ensure your event loop never stops. Arcane Initiate is also excellent to dig through Agatha's massive 35-card deck to find your spells.  

### Events to Loop (Discard Pile)
* Read the Signs (Level 0–2): Your premier investigate tool. Play it normally to get 2 clues, then recur it at the end of the turn to get 2 more clues.  
* Drawn to the Flame: Testless clue gathering that you can play twice in a round if you have the sanity soak.  
* Spectral Shield: Gives you immense defensive safety, allowing you to easily shrug off enemy attacks or encounter damage.  

## 2. The Chaos-Bag Dictator (The "Olive Loop")
If you prefer traditional Mystic token-manipulation, this build leans heavily into bending the chaos bag to your will, utilizing standard Mystic tech alongside Seeker testless events.  

### The Core Engine
* The Key Asset: Olive McBride. Because Olive forces you to reveal 3 tokens, choose 2 to resolve, and ignore 1, activating her on a test immediately opens up Agatha's end-of-turn window.  
* Bag Fixers: Ritual Candles or Grotesque Statue. Olive can make tests risky since she draws more tokens. Ritual Candles mitigate the sting of symbol tokens, while Grotesque Statue gives you absolute control over what gets ignored and what gets resolved.  
* Signature Asset Priority: You must prioritize finding your Ocula Obscura. Because Agatha’s deck size is 35, include Backpack or Seeker draw power like Deep Knowledge and Preposterous Sketches to locate it early. Once Ocula is down, sealing number tokens ensures you always pass your tests.  

### Events to Loop (Discard Pile)
* Voice of Ra: Because you are intentionally drawing extra tokens via Olive McBride, Voice of Ra turns into an absolute gold mine, often netting you 4 to 5 resources per play—which you can then do again at the end of your turn.
* String of Curses: Agatha can drop an enemy, place a doom, play String of Curses from her hand to instantly defeat a non-elite enemy, and then recur it at the end of her turn to wipe out a second foe.
* Working a Hunch: Testless, fast clue compression that bypasses nasty high-shroud locations entirely.

## Pilot Pro-Tip: Mind the Timing Window
The biggest trap for new Agatha players is trying to use her recursion for a mid-turn combat burst. Because her ability triggers specifically in the end-of-turn window, offensive spells like Spectral Razor or Shrivelling played via her reaction won't help you clear an enemy before your turn ends.  

Instead, use your actual actions during your turn to fight or investigate, and use the end-of-turn trigger to play defensive, utility, or testless clue events (Spectral Shield, Working a Hunch, or Deep Knowledge) to prep yourself for the Mythos phase
